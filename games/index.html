Games

Since my childhood, games have been my passion. For few years, I am not only playing games - I develop them as well. Roguelikes are my all-time favorite, and I participated in many editions of 7 Day Roguelike Challenge game jam.

Below, I will present you all games I created since 2013.

Rogue Robs Trains

  stable     game jam     7drl  

Rogue Robs Trains is fast paced roguelike created for 7 Day Roguelike Challenge 2019.
Player is a bandit that boarded the train to get his hands on gold filled chests!
I wanted to catch the spaghetti western atmosphere and provide satisfying ranged combat mechanics.
It is the first game created with RAWIG, and I hope not the last one - it worked very well and sped up the development process significantly. I had to adapt framework for different needs, but most of changes made specifically for Rogue Robs Trains has been implemented into RAWIG later.


Unnamed Broughlike

  alpha  

It is my most recent game-related project.
I was reading about broughlikes recently. It is very specific genre, related to roguelikes, but everything started with Michael Brough. Linked wikipedia article provides rough overview of design goals of broughlikes. Unfortunately, I do not have access to iOS devices, but I read a lot about Brough's games.
I have to say, I like their premise: small scale and streamlined gameplay, yet every run is full of meaningful decisions.
My take on broughlikes is still in early stage of development, but I had already a lot of fun with designing basic mechanics. I want to make resources management the core of the game, yet keep it simple. Player - pilot of mecha - can shoot foes from afar, but every enemy is vulnerable to only one type of damage. Launchers' magazines are short, therefore player need to replenish ammo often. The catch? Every drained resource should raise risk level, for example by spawning new enemy.
